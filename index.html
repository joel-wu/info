Research

I am interested in developing systems proficient in the acquisition, reconstruction, analysis, and interpretation of information.

Presently, my work involves spectral reconstruction, computational imaging, and physics-informed AI. I am also interested in edge AI and open to other topics.

My previous experience includes vision and remote sensing projects, where I contributed to:

  1. Subpixel level interpretation of multi-spectral satellite maps, crucial for land resource management, geological disaster monitoring, and urban planning.

  2. Multi-modal generation of hyperspectral images, providing valuable and reliable training data for data-driven models.
Local-then-Global Spatial Semantic-Aware Network for Subpixel Mapping
Yuheng Wu, Yunyi Wang, Erting Pan, Yang Yu, Xiaoguang Mei
in submission to IEEE Transactions on Geoscience and Remote Sensing

Introduce a new local-then-local attention mechanism to understand hyperspectral data. Explore a semantic-aware paradigm for subpixel mapping.

Unmixing before Fusion: A Generalized Paradigm for Multi-modality-based Hyperspectral Image Synthesis
Yang Yu, Erting Pan, Xinya Wang, Yuheng Wu, Xiaoguang Mei, Jiayi Ma
in submission to CVPR2024

Introduce a new paradigm for hyperspectral image synthesis. Generate reliable data from abundance domain.
